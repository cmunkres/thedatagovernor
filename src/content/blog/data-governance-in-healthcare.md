## What Is Data Governance?

Data governance is the process of setting rules and regulations for how data can be used within an organization. It includes developing standards for how data will be collected, stored, and accessed.

The goal is to ensure that everyone in the organization is using the same set of data and that it is accurate, secure, and consistent. This helps ensure that everyone has access to up-to-date information they can trust.

One way to think of data governance is as a sort of "traffic cop" for your organization's data flow. By establishing clear rules about what types of information are allowed where you can avoid having multiple versions of the same information floating around which could lead to confusion or even errors.

Additionally, by defining roles and responsibilities related to [data governance,](/data-governance-framework-a-holistic-approach-to-data-management/) you can ensure that everyone knows who is responsible for what when it comes to managing your organization's data.

## How Data Governance is used in Healthcare

Data governance has numerous benefits for healthcare organizations. For one thing, it can help reduce costs by eliminating redundant processes or unnecessary paperwork.

Additionally, it helps organizations become more agile by allowing them to quickly respond to changes in demand or regulations without having to go back through mounds of paperwork or outdated systems.

Finally, by helping organizations make better decisions based on timely and accurate information, it can also increase patient safety and satisfaction levels.

## HIPPA Role in Data Governance

HIPAA plays a key role in data governance. During the transmission or storage of patient information, HIPAA requires that all safeguards be taken to keep protected health information (PHI) confidential and secure. 

Any organization dealing with individuals' protected health information must abide by HIPAA regulations as it provides standards for how PHI should be shared and protect individual privacy. Although HIPAA is not the only legal framework governing how PHI is kept secure, it sets a precedence for [data security and data governance](/?p=1553) that organizations must take into account if they want to be compliant. 

HIPAA controls who can access this data and ensures mandates are followed around what types of steps an organization needs to follow when handling patient personal information such as authentication, authorization, and access control procedures. 

Together with other minimum requirements set forth by HIPAA, organizations will find themselves being able to monitor their decentralized networks more efficiently while avoiding potential risks and liabilities due to mishandling personal information.

## Conclusion

Overall, implementing a strong system of effective data governance in healthcare will benefit both patients and providers alike. By ensuring that everyone in the organization is using up-to-date information from reliable sources —and not relying on outdated or inaccurate sources —you can help improve decision-making throughout your organization while also reducing costs associated with duplicate processes or paperwork errors. So don't let the term scare you; data governance isn't nearly as boring as it sounds—it's actually quite useful!

And remember, having a system of data governance in place is just one part of an effective healthcare organization —you will still need to ensure that your processes are compliant with all relevant regulations and that everyone in the organization understands their role when it comes to handling data. With the right approach, you can create a culture where everyone works together to ensure better outcomes for patients and more efficient operations overall.

So what are you waiting for? Start implementing a data governance strategy today and reap the rewards! Your organization and your patients will thank you.
