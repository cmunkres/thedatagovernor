## What does a data scientist do day to day?

The role of a data scientist is to take raw data and turn it into actionable insights. This can involve a variety of tasks, such as:

* Collecting and cleaning data from various sources: Data scientists need to have a good understanding of data structure, data quality, and data governance. They may need to work with different types of data, such as structured and unstructured data, and need to have the skills to clean and format data in a way that can be used for analysis.
* Exploring and visualizing data: Data scientists need to have the ability to understand patterns and trends in data by using various data visualization techniques. They may use tools like Tableau, Power BI, or Python libraries like Matplotlib and Seaborn to create visualizations that help to identify patterns and outliers in the data.
* Building models: Data scientists use statistical techniques and machine learning algorithms to build predictive models. These models can be used to make predictions or identify relationships in the data. Data scientists need to have a good understanding of different types of models, such as linear regression, decision trees, and neural networks and be able to select the appropriate model for a given problem.
* Communicating findings: Data scientists need to be able to communicate their findings to non-technical stakeholders. This can involve creating reports and presentations, as well as interacting with business leaders to explain the results of the analysis and help them make data-driven decisions.

## What skills are needed to be a data scientist?

To be a successful [data scientist](/data-engineer-vs-data-scientist-the-dynamic-duo-of-data-management-and-analysis/), you will need a combination of technical and soft skills. Some of the key technical skills needed include:

* Strong programming skills: Data scientists typically use programming languages like Python or R to clean, manipulate, and analyze data. They should have a good understanding of programming concepts like variables, functions, and loops, and be able to write clean and efficient code.
* Knowledge of statistical techniques and machine learning algorithms: Data scientists use statistical techniques and machine learning algorithms to build predictive models. They should have a good understanding of different types of models and be able to select the appropriate model for a given problem.
* Familiarity with databases and SQL: Data scientists often work with large data sets that are stored in databases. They should have a good understanding of database concepts and be able to write SQL queries to extract data from databases.
* Experience with data visualization tools: Data scientists use data visualization tools to create visualizations that help to identify patterns and outliers in the data. They should have experience with tools like Tableau, Power BI, or Python libraries like Matplotlib and Seaborn.

## Is it hard to become a data scientist?

In addition to technical skills, data scientists should have strong problem-solving skills and the ability to think creatively. They should also be able to communicate their findings to non-technical stakeholders, as they will often be working closely with business leaders to inform decisions.

Data science is a multidisciplinary field that requires a broad range of knowledge and skills. Data scientists need to have a good understanding of statistics, computer science, and the domain they are working in. They also need to be able to work well in teams, as they often collaborate with other data scientists, engineers, and business leaders.

Being a data scientist is a challenging but rewarding career. It requires a combination of technical and soft skills, and the ability to think creatively and communicate effectively. 

## Do data scientists get paid a lot?

The salary for a data scientist can vary depending on factors such as location, industry, and level of experience. On average, data scientists in the United States can expect to earn a salary in the range of $80,000 to $150,000 per year, with the median salary being around $120,000 per year. 

However, the salary can be higher for experienced data scientists or those working in certain industries such as technology or finance. According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a data scientist in the United States is around $116,000 per year. 

It's worth noting that the salary also varies depending on the level of experience, a data scientist with just a few years of experience might earn less than a senior data scientist with many years of experience. Additionally, some data scientists may earn bonuses or other forms of compensation on top of their base salary. 

## Conclusion

Becoming a data scientist requires a combination of technical and soft skills, as well as a deep understanding of statistics, computer science, and the domain they are working in. The path to becoming a data scientist can be challenging, but with the right skills and experience, it can be a rewarding career with a significant salary.
