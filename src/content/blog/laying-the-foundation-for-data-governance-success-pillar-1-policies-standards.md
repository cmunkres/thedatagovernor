Effective [data governance policy](/what-is-data-governance-and-why-do-we-need-it/) starts with a well-defined set of policies & standards. These policies and standards provide the foundation for a comprehensive data governance program, serving as a roadmap for how data should be managed and used within an organization. In this deep dive, we'll explore the importance of policy and standards in data governance and what they should encompass.

## Why Policy and Standards are Essential

Policies and standards play a crucial role in data governance by setting expectations for how data should be managed and used within an organization. They provide a clear understanding of the responsibilities and obligations of [different stakeholders and ensure that data](/data-engineer-vs-data-scientist-the-dynamic-duo-of-data-management-and-analysis/) is used ethically and competently. 

Organizations can reduce the risk of data breaches, non-compliance, and other security incidents by having well-defined policies & standards, increasing efficiency, and improving overall data quality.

## What to Include in Policies and Standards

When developing policies and standards for data governance, it's essential to consider the following:

1. Data Management: Policies and standards should outline the processes and procedures for collecting, storing, and maintaining data, as well as who is responsible for managing it.
2. Data Access: Policies and standards should specify who is authorized to access data and how data can be used. This includes guidelines for data security and data protection.
3. Data Quality: Policies and standards should establish metrics for data quality and outline the processes for ensuring data accuracy and completeness.
4. Data Retention: Policies and standards should specify how long data should be retained and when it should be disposed of.
5. Compliance: Policies and standards should ensure that the organization complies with relevant regulations and standards, such as data privacy laws and industry-specific regulations.

Incorporating policies and standards into data governance is the first step towards ensuring that data is managed effectively and used appropriately. By following best practices for policy and standards development, organizations can build a strong foundation for data governance success.

## Conclusion

Policies and standards are the foundation of a comprehensive data governance program. They clearly understand how [data should be managed](/how-to-create-efficient-master-data-management-workflows-and-approvals/) and used, reducing the risk of security incidents and ensuring compliance with relevant regulations. 

By developing effective policies and standards, organizations can position themselves for success in today's data-driven world.
