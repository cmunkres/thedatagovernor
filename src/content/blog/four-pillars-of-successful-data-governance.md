In today's digital age, data has become one of the most valuable assets a company can possess. From customer information to financial records, data is essential to the success of any business. Companies must establish a robust [data governance framework](/what-is-data-governance-and-why-do-we-need-it/) system to manage this valuable resource.

The Data Management Association (DAMA) provides guidelines for effective data governance, including four key pillars that support successful data management.

## Pillar 1: Policy and Standards

The first pillar of data governance is policy and standards. This pillar establishes guidelines for data management and usage to ensure consistent and efficient use of data.

Policies and standards should be developed with input from relevant stakeholders and should be designed to align with the company's overall goals and objectives.

One of the key benefits of having a clear set of policies and standards is that it helps to minimize the [risk of data](/using-data-for-risk-management/) breaches and other security incidents.

For example, policies and standards may specify who has access to sensitive data, how that data is stored, and how it can be used. These policies and standards should also address the protection of personal information, such as customer data, and provide guidance on handling data breaches.

## Pillar 2: Stewardship

The second pillar of data governance is stewardship. This pillar involves designating responsible parties for ensuring data quality, accuracy, completeness, and compliance with data policies and standards.

Stewardship can be carried out by individuals or committees, depending on the size of the organization and the complexity of its data management needs.

One of the main objectives of stewardship is to promote a culture of [data-driven decision](/business-intelligence-the-key-to-making-data-driven-decisions/) making within the organization. This requires a commitment to data quality and accuracy, as well as a focus on data literacy.

Stewardship is also responsible for ensuring that data is used appropriately and that it is protected against unauthorized access and use.

## Pillar 3: Metrics and Measurement

The third pillar of data governance is metrics and measurement. This pillar involves establishing methods for measuring the effectiveness of data management practices and for continuous improvement.

Metrics and measurements are essential for demonstrating [data governance's](/laying-the-foundation-for-data-governance-success-pillar-1-policies-standards/) value to stakeholders and tracking progress toward data management goals.

Examples of metrics and measurements that can be used to evaluate data governance include data quality metrics, data availability metrics, and data security metrics.

By regularly tracking these metrics, organizations can identify areas for improvement and make necessary changes to their data management practices.

## Pillar 4: Architecture and Technology

The final pillar of data governance is architecture and technology. This pillar involves implementing data management technologies and practices that support data governance policies, standards, and objectives.

The architecture and technology used for data management should be chosen based on the company's specific needs and should be designed to meet the needs of all relevant stakeholders.

Technologies and practices supporting data governance include data management platforms, data warehousing, data quality tools, and data security measures.

By implementing the right architecture and technology, companies can ensure that their [data is managed effectively](/data-visualization-for-data-science-a-guide-to-effective-communication-of-data/) and that they are able to make informed decisions based on accurate and reliable data.

## Conclusion

By following the four pillars of data governance outlined by DAMA, organizations can effectively manage their data and ensure that it is used appropriately and protected against unauthorized access and use.

This can help organizations to make informed decisions based on accurate and reliable [data while also reducing the risk](/using-data-for-risk-management/) of data breaches and other security incidents.

Implementing a data governance program can also help organizations to increase efficiency and improve overall data quality. This can be achieved through regular monitoring of data quality metrics and continuous improvement initiatives aimed at addressing any areas of weakness.

In addition to the benefits outlined above, a robust data governance program can also help organizations to establish trust with customers and other stakeholders.

By demonstrating a commitment to data quality and security, organizations can build a reputation for reliability and credibility, which can be a valuable asset in a competitive business environment.

Data governance is an essential aspect of successful data management. By following the four pillars of data governance outlined by DAMA, organizations can effectively manage their data, reduce risk, and build a competitive advantage.
